Brayden Noh

California Institute of Technology | snoh@caltech.edu

Hi, I’m Brayden, a senior at Caltech studying Earth science and computational modeling. My work focuses on coupling surface and subsurface processes to quantitatively understand landscape evolution and natural hazards.

I’ve previously worked with Michael Lamb, Omar Wani, Karl Lang, and Vinamra Agrawal on projects ranging from asteroid impact simulations to geomorphic probabilistic risk modeling.

Starting this fall, I’ll be pursuing a PhD in Earth and Planetary Sciences at Harvard, where I’ll be advised by Brendan Meade.
